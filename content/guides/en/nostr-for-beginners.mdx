This guide will get you up and running on Nostr in about ten minutes. No technical background required. By the end, you'll have your own identity, a working app, and people to follow.

## Step 1: Understand What You're Creating

On Nostr, your identity is a **key pair** — a public key and a private key. Think of it this way:

- **Public key (npub):** Your address. Share it freely. People use it to find and follow you.
- **Private key (nsec):** Your master password. It proves you own your identity. Never share it with anyone.

There is no email registration. No phone number. No company holding your account. You generate these keys, and they are yours permanently.

<Callout type="warning">
Most Nostr apps — including the Nostr WoT Extension — generate a 12-word seed phrase instead of showing you the raw private key. Write down those 12 words and store them somewhere safe — a password manager or a piece of paper in a secure location. If you lose it, there is no "forgot password" option. No one can recover it for you.
</Callout>

## Step 2: Pick a Client

A "client" is just an app that lets you use Nostr. The same way you can check email with Gmail, Outlook, or Apple Mail, you can use Nostr with different clients. They all connect to the same network.

Here are solid options to start with:

**On desktop (web browser):**
- [Primal](https://primal.net) — Clean interface, easy for newcomers. Works in any browser.
- [Coracle](https://coracle.social) — Lightweight, focused on simplicity.

**On mobile:**
- [Primal](https://primal.net/downloads) — Available for iOS and Android.
- [Damus](https://damus.io) — iOS only. One of the most popular Nostr apps.
- [Amethyst](https://github.com/vitorpamplona/amethyst) — Android only. Full-featured.

Pick one and sign up. Most will offer to generate your keys during the setup process.

<Callout type="tip">
Starting with Primal in a web browser is the easiest path. You can always switch to another client later — your identity and posts come with you.
</Callout>

## Step 3: Set Up Your Profile

Once you have your keys, fill in your profile. This is what people see when they find you:

- **Display name** — Your real name or a pseudonym. Your choice.
- **Bio** — A sentence or two about who you are.
- **Avatar** — A profile picture. Optional but recommended.
- **NIP-05 identifier** — A verified address like `you@yourdomain.com`. Optional. You can set this up later.

Keep it simple. You can always update these later.

## Step 4: Connect to Relays

Relays are the servers that store and deliver your posts. Most clients come pre-configured with a default set of relays, so you probably don't need to change anything right away.

If you want to check or adjust your relays:

1. Open your client's settings.
2. Look for a "Relays" section.
3. Make sure you're connected to at least 3–5 relays for good coverage.

Popular relays include `wss://relay.damus.io`, `wss://relay.primal.net`, and `wss://nos.lol`. Your client likely connects to these or similar ones by default.

<Callout type="tip">
You don't need to understand relays in depth to get started. Just make sure your client is connected to a few. You can fine-tune this later as you learn more.
</Callout>

![Your client connects to multiple independent relay servers — your posts are stored across all of them simultaneously](/images/guides/nostr-for-beginners/relays-diagram.svg)

## Step 5: Follow People

Nostr is better with a feed. Here's how to find people:

- **Search by name** in your client's search bar.
- **Paste an npub** (public key) directly to find a specific person.
- **Browse trending posts** — most clients have a discover or trending section.
- **Check who others follow** — find one person you know and look at their follow list.

## Step 6: Post Something

You're set up. Say hello. The Nostr community actively welcomes newcomers.

Your post goes out to your connected relays. Anyone following you — or browsing those relays — can see it. No algorithm decides who sees your content.

## What's Next

You now have a working Nostr identity and a feed to browse. From here, a few things worth exploring:

- **Web of Trust:** As you follow people and they follow you back, you're building a trust network. The [Nostr WoT Extension](https://nostr-wot.com) helps you visualize and manage this — it's useful once you have some connections, but no rush.
- **Zaps:** Nostr supports instant Bitcoin micropayments called "zaps." You can tip posts you like. This requires a Lightning wallet, which you can set up whenever you're ready.
- **Multiple clients:** Try different apps. Your identity works across all of them. Find the one that fits how you use social media.

The most important thing: Nostr is a protocol, not a platform. There's no algorithm to game, no company to please, and no single point of failure. Your presence here is yours to build — and yours to keep.
